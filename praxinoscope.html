Praxinoscope 幻燈箱機械裝置

“Praxinoscope x Gear Train” is my first project at MakingOnLoft (It is part of a bigger project at MOL - details here.)

The requirement was to make a mechanical device that plays the cartoons drawn by some students from Hong Chi Morninghope School. Our team decided to use praxinoscopes.

As the main designer and engineer, I decided to make it a bit more challenging - all praxinoscopes are mechanically connected using gears and are driven by two motors. The whole train of gears is also flexible to be bent into different shapes, thus has the ability to adapt to different environments.

The philosophy behind was 'be water' - be as flexible and as adaptable as water. I also used as much wood as possible, instead of plastic and metal, to give it a natural feel, and to be more environmentally friendly.
