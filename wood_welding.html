Wood Welding Research Project 木焊接研究

木焊接是碩士課程的研究項目。

記得最初接觸木焊接的時候，由心而發「哇」了一聲。兩塊木頭，不用膠水，不用釘子，只需施加一定的壓力，配合適當的振動頻率、幅度和時間，就可以神奇地黏在一起，接合面的強度甚至可與原本的木材比擬；既環保，又快捷。「一直夢寐以求的魔法啊」，我心想。

這項研究的目的是：分析木結構在焊接過程中的轉變，以及其對接合面的影響；希望有朝一日，這項技術可以成熟到得以普及使用。

The Welding Institute (TWI)是這個項目的合作夥伴。他們提供了機器和機器的技術支援。成功進行焊接後，樣本在劍橋大學工程系的實驗室進行分析和測試。

可惜，由於我在2020年的12月退學，最後只能見證第一階段的測試。這個研究也許是我想要完成碩士課程的唯一動力──教授和同事都是很可愛的人，而研究木焊接又是難得的機會。話雖如此，我最後依然決然離開。

My final year project.

"Wood welding" is a novel way of bonding wood. To me, it's like magic - two pieces of wood are joined directly using linear friction welding without any adhesive or even thermoplastic!

The aim of the project was to elucidate the microscopic structural changes caused by welding and their effects on the strength of the joint, so that this technology could be brought into practical applications.

The project was support by The Welding Institute (TWI) - they provided the machine to carry out welding. Subsequent material analysis and testing were done at the Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge.

Unfortunately, I have stopped working on the project after the first phase of welding and testing because I dropped out of university in December 2020. It was enjoyable working on this project - my supervisor, project partner and colleagues from TWI were all incrediblely nice people. In fact, this project was the only thing that made me wanted to stay and finish the degree. I still decided to quit, however, for life is short and I felt the urge to follow my passion.
